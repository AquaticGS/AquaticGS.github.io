Aquatic-GS:A Hybrid 3D Representation for Underwater Scenes

Beihang University

Abstract

3D representation of underwater scenes is a valuable yet challenging task due to the distance-dependent and wavelength-selective attenuation and scattering effects. These water effects cause even the most advanced 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) methods to struggle with effectively representing underwater scenes, resulting in unrealistic colors and incorrect geometry.

In this study, we propose Aquatic-GS, a novel hybrid 3D representation approach for underwater scenes. Specifically, we construct a Neural Water Field (NWF) to implicitly model the water properties, while employing the 3DGS to explicitly model the water-free scene (i.e., without the influence of the water medium). The outputs of both components are then combined through a physics-based underwater image formation model, enabling the rendering of underwater scenes. To tackle the challenges water effects pose in accurately representing scene geometry and details, we design a Depth-Guided Optimization (DGO) mechanism that uses a pseudo-depth map to guide optimization for more precise scene representation. After optimization, Aquatic-GS not only enables the rendering of novel underwater viewpoints but also supports restoring the true appearance of underwater scenes, as if the water medium were absent.

Extensive experiments on both simulated and real-world datasets demonstrate that our Aquatic-GS surpasses existing NeRF-based underwater representations, achieving better rendering quality and real-time rendering performance with a 270x increase in speed. Furthermore, in terms of underwater scene restoration, our Aquatic-GS outperforms representative dewatering methods in visual fidelity, detail recovery, color correction, and stability.
